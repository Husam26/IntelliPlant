# SOP: Mechanical Seal Replacement for Pump P-101

## 1. Overview
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the steps required to safely and effectively replace the mechanical seal on Centrifugal Pump P-101. The mechanical seal prevents fluid leakage along the pump shaft. 
Frequent seal failures on P-101 have been linked to improper installation and misalignment.

## 2. Safety Precautions
- **Lockout/Tagout (LOTO):** Ensure power to the pump motor is completely disconnected, locked, and tagged.
- **De-pressurize:** Isolate the pump using suction and discharge valves. Drain the pump casing completely.
- **PPE:** Wear safety goggles, chemical-resistant gloves, and steel-toed boots.

## 3. Required Tools & Materials
- New Mechanical Seal Kit (Part #MSK-204)
- Allen wrench set
- Torque wrench
- Dial indicator (for shaft alignment)
- Lint-free cloths and O-ring lubricant

## 4. Procedure

### Step 4.1: Disassembly
1. Remove the coupling guard and disconnect the motor coupling.
2. Unbolt and remove the pump casing cover.
3. Carefully slide the impeller off the shaft (use an impeller puller if necessary).
4. Remove the old mechanical seal assembly from the seal chamber.

### Step 4.2: Inspection and Cleaning
1. Inspect the pump shaft for scoring, wear, or bending. Max acceptable runout is 0.002 inches.
2. Clean the seal chamber and shaft thoroughly using a lint-free cloth and approved solvent. Ensure no debris remains.

### Step 4.3: Installation of New Seal
1. Lightly lubricate the new O-rings with the supplied lubricant. Do NOT touch the carbon/ceramic seal faces with bare hands.
2. Slide the stationary part of the seal into the gland plate.
3. Carefully slide the rotary part of the seal onto the shaft until it seats against the shaft shoulder.
4. Reinstall the gland plate and tighten the bolts evenly in a crisscross pattern to the specified torque (15 ft-lbs).

### Step 4.4: Reassembly
1. Reinstall the impeller and casing cover.
2. Reconnect the motor coupling and perform a laser alignment to ensure the motor and pump shafts are perfectly aligned.
3. Reinstall the coupling guard.

## 5. Post-Maintenance Testing
1. Open suction and discharge valves to flood the pump. Vent any trapped air.
2. Check for static leaks around the seal area.
3. Remove LOTO and restore power.
4. Start the pump and monitor for unusual vibrations, noise, or dynamic leakage for at least 15 minutes.
